CARTOGRAHPY PORTFOLIO

During summer term at UCU I took a Geographical Information Systems (GIS) course. This website is my online portfolio, showing off the maps I created. This course was taught by Britta Rickert, from the Copernicus institute of sustainable development. During the two weeks of this course, I learned a lot of practical skills, working with many different GIS forms and learning to interpret the data and maps. I learned to work with different data and file types (Tiff, Shapefile, Json, etc) Furthermore, we worked with many softwares, such as ArcGIS online, ArcGIS pro, QGIS and Google Earth Engine.

What Is GIS?

Geographical information systems (GIS) are used to visualize and analyse data. Maps are important to convey large amounts of information to many people, in a relatively simple way. This can be of importance for, among others, goverments and policy makers.
